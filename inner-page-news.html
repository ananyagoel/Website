ALCIFO demonstrates the business use-case of regional air mobility market in Florida in their recently released white paper

10 October, 2022

A recently published white paper from ALCIFO describes the complex decision-making process of establishing a viable business use-case for air taxis. “Building a novel aircraft technology that is certifiable — which by itself is a very difficult task — is about looking at only one aspect of the multidimensional advanced air mobility ecosystem. There are other sides — equally important — that require understanding of the performance of the aircraft from an operator’s perspective,” said the author of the white paper Dr. Satadru Roy, the founder and CEO of ALCIFO. The white paper demonstrates the business use-cases of the Eviation’s Alice-like aircraft in Florida for Regional Air Mobility (RAM) market under different seat configurations and air taxi fares.

Aircraft design, network operations, market demand, economics, and infrastructure — some of the main pillars of the advanced air mobility ecosystem — are highly inter-wined. “There exists interdependency between the aircraft design and the network it operates on and this interdependency is stronger when the size of the aircraft is small. Imagine a 180-seat Boeing 737-800 aircraft and if a handful of its seats are flying empty majority of the times, it does not affect much the performance of the fleet of aircraft on the network they are operated. However, imagine you are building a small 5-seat aircraft and if just one of the seats is consistently flying empty, despite the operator’s effort to incentivize its customer by various means to fill up that seat, the aircraft is probably over-designed and there is a room to further optimize the design and significantly improve its performance,” Roy added, to emphasize the need of the data-driven holistic approach and the need to streamline the complex decision-making process related to network analysis that these aircraft manufacturers’ often encounter.

A couple of years back, the director of engineering at Kittyhawk — an air taxi startup founded by the pioneer of self-driving car Sebastian Thrun and backed by Google’s co-founder Larry Page — reached out to Satadru (he was working as a research faculty at GeorgiaTech back then), asking if he could help evaluate the performance of their aircraft from an operator’s perspective using a detailed network analysis — given his domain expertise and something he has been working on for about a decade now. But due to his restrictive work visa and his work commitment, he had to pass on the request. Fast forward today, ALCIFO is formed with the mission to provide a thorough understanding of the entire air taxi ecosystem via a high-fidelity network simulation capability. “If ALCIFO was incorporated back then, Kittyhawk would have been our first customer,” said Roy.
